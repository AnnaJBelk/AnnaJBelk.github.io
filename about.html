Biography

Mathematics, Chemistry, Biochemistry, and Software Development are not typical pursuits for a young girl growing up in a beautiful, serene valley near the Ouachita Mountains of Arkansas. But I have never been a "typical" girl.

In the quiet beauty of a valley untouched by telephones, television, or modern distractions, I developed a curiosity that propelled me beyond those green mountains to the campus of Arkansas Tech University. There, I found new beauty in Chemistry and Mathematics and began to discover myself. Science defines me and shapes my worldview.

As the Information Age ignited, I graduated summa cum laude with a BS in Chemistry and Mathematics. Alongside my studies, I taught myself programming languages like C, FORTRAN, Pascal, and Transact. At Phillips Phillips Petroleum Co., my first employer, I helped invent an electronic notebook for the catalyst design group.

The rise of the Biotechnology Age inspired me to pursue further studies at Purdue University, where I completed the core Biochemistry curriculum. Later, I moved west to UCLA to conduct PhD-level research in Computational and Theoretical Physical Organic Chemistry.

Today, I work at TransMedia Inc., where I maintain a suite of video games for legal education. These pioneering games are among the first to be powered by artificial intelligence.
